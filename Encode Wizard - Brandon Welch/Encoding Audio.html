Encoding audio is a simple process in which you determine the right quality while

making it compatable with most players. Now of course not all players honor the

5.1 soundtrack that is in most players. There are two options for making it still

playable across all players. One option is to set the bitrate to whatever one you want.

If you are going to use this option, most likely you want to use the 384 option. The

second option is to use the quality setting instead which is probably the most preferred.

Why you probably want to use the quality option compared to the bitrate is

because the quality option is much more flexable and is more closer to the original

source. The bitrate option often can be let down in loud instances or very quiet instances

or could have sounds that sound "watery" or not as clear. When using the quality setting,

it is prefered that you set it to 5 for AC-3 5.1 tracks or AC-3 7.1 tracks as a source. For DTS

5.1 or AC-3 2.0 tracks, use 2.
